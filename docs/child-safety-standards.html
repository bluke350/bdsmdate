Child Safety Standards

App: BDSM Date and Hookups

Last updated: April 15, 2026

Our commitment

BDSM Date and Hookups is committed to the safety of children and takes any suggestion of child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) or child sexual abuse material (CSAM) extremely seriously. We strictly prohibit any sexual content involving minors in our applications and services.

Key policies

  • Minimum user age: 18+. Users must confirm they are at least 18 years old when registering.
  • Zero-tolerance for sexual content involving minors: accounts, images, messages, or profiles that depict or solicit sexual activity with minors are prohibited.
  • We remove content and suspend or terminate accounts that violate these policies and will preserve evidence to assist law enforcement.
  • We comply with applicable regional and national laws regarding CSAE/CSAM and will report incidents to the appropriate authorities.

How to report (to us)

If you encounter content, profiles, or messages that you believe involve a minor or CSAM, please report it immediately using one of the following methods:

  1. In-app reporting: Use the "Report" control on the user's profile or the message thread to submit a report and select the "Child safety" or equivalent category.
  2. Email our designated child-safety contact: wildbills1977@gmail.com

If someone is in immediate danger, contact local emergency services first. We also encourage reporting to local law enforcement and, where relevant, national reporting centers (see links below).

How we handle reports

After a report is received we will:

  • Immediately review and, where appropriate, remove or restrict access to the reported content or account.
  • Preserve relevant data and logs (timestamps, account identifiers, message content where applicable, IPs and metadata) to assist investigations.
  • Suspend or terminate accounts that violate our policies.
  • Where required by law or to prevent ongoing harm, report confirmed or credible CSAM/CSAE incidents to law enforcement and national reporting centers (for example, in the U.S. we report to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children / report.cybertip.org).
  • Cooperate with law enforcement and provide information to help investigations, subject to legal process and privacy laws.

Prevention measures

  • Age gating and clear registration flows that require users to affirm they are 18+.
  • Automated detection tools and manual moderation to identify suspected CSAM/CSAE content.
  • User reporting tools accessible from profiles and message threads.
  • Account verification and escalation processes for high-risk reports.
  • Staff training on responding to CSAE/CSAM reports and evidence preservation.
